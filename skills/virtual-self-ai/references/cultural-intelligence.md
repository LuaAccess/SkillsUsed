# Cultural Intelligence Reference — Filipino / Asian Business Context

Apply automatically in every MODE 1 debrief and every MODE 2 scenario. Do not wait to be asked.


## Signal Decoder Table

| Signal Observed | Surface Reading | Real Meaning — Apply This |
|----------------|----------------|--------------------------|
| "We'll circle back" | Delay, rescheduling | Soft no OR unresolved internal conflict — treat as stall until proven otherwise |
| "We'll think about it" | Consideration | Polite decline or gatekeeper blocking — probe the internal dynamic |
| "Very interesting" (no follow-up question) | Engagement | Polite deflection — they are not engaged enough to ask more |
| Excessive nodding + agreement with no specifics | Buy-in | Surface compliance, not commitment — they haven't said yes internally yet |
| Extended small talk / off-topic conversation | Inefficiency | Active trust-building — in PH business culture, this IS the meeting. Do not cut it short. |
| Silent senior attendee | Observer | Evaluator — their reaction matters more than anyone who spoke |
| Indirect reference to problems with past vendor | Background context | Direct statement of current pain — this is the real requirement, stated sideways |
| Code-switch to Filipino mid-sentence (from English) | Normal conversation | Emotional register shift — something significant was just said or felt |
| Formal sudden shift from warm tone | Style preference | Relationship cooling, political sensitivity, or discomfort with direction |
| Two or more cancelled meetings without rescheduling | Scheduling conflict | Declining interest — treat as soft disqualification signal |
| Client introduces you to more internal people | Trust signal | Pipeline advancing — they are building internal support for you |
| Client stops introducing you to internal people | Busy period | Gating access — they are reassessing your fit |
| Laughter + rapport without agenda progress | Unproductive meeting | Highest trust signal — this is how PH clients signal comfort. Business follows. |


## Power Structure Mapping — Philippine Enterprise

### How Real Power Flows (often ≠ org chart)

```
Who controls BUDGET → Finance head, CFO, or business unit owner (not IT)
Who controls ACCESS → IT head, CTO, or trusted internal champion
Who controls TIMING → Procurement, compliance, or the CFO's EA
Who controls NARRATIVE → The person who briefs the decision-maker before the meeting
```

Always ask: who in this account is briefing the approver *after* you leave the room?

### Hierarchy Signals in Meetings

- Who speaks **first** → sets the agenda the group wants to follow
- Who speaks **last** → often has the most real authority
- Who **defers** → maps reporting lines and political caution
- Who stays **silent** → either irrelevant or the most important person in the room
- Who **laughs first** → often the informal cultural authority, not the org chart authority


## Communication Style Calibration

### Filipino Business Communication Norms

| Norm | Implication for Luther |
|------|----------------------|
| Indirect refusal | Never interpret silence or vague language as neutral — decode it |
| Saving face | Clients will not admit they don't understand, that a past vendor failed them, or that they made a bad decision — they will tell you *sideways* |
| Relationship precedes transaction | A meeting with no decision is not a wasted meeting — it may be a trust-building meeting, which is more valuable |
| Hierarchy respect | Addressing the most junior person in a group before the most senior is a misstep — read the room |
| Pakikisama (group harmony) | Disagreement is rare in front of others — real objections surface in 1:1 or after the meeting |

### Code-Switching as Intelligence Signal

When a Filipino client or contact switches from English to Filipino (or Taglish) mid-sentence:
- It marks a **moment of genuine emotion** — comfort, frustration, emphasis, or real opinion
- What they say in Filipino after the switch is often the most honest thing said in the meeting
- Do not treat this as casual — it is data
- Luther should match the language shift when he has the relationship to do so — it signals respect and belonging


## Cultural Power Dynamics in Competitive Situations

- Replacing an incumbent in PH enterprise requires giving the client a **face-saving narrative** — not just a better offer. They need a story for why they switched that doesn't imply they made a mistake before.
- Relationship history between the client and a competitor is **not just a barrier** — it is information. What does that relationship tell you about what the client values?
- In government and quasi-government: **process compliance** often matters more than the best solution. Winning requires understanding the procurement narrative, not just the technical evaluation.
- **Brand prestige** carries real weight in PH enterprise — especially banking, insurance, and conglomerates. Counter this by reframing brand as risk ("Large SIs rotate resources").
